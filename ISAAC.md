## The Freemium Modelüàö
Offers a basic version of the AI application for free and charge users for premium features.
### Example
### Spotify
Spotify is a music streaming platform that offers a free version with limited features, such as ads and limited skips, and a premium version with additional features, such as ad-free listening and offline playback.
### Dropbox
Dropbox is a cloud-based file storage and sharing service that offers a free version with limited storage space and a premium version with more storage and additional features.
### LinkedIn
LinkedIn is a professional networking platform that offers a free version with limited features, such as a limited number of profile views and messages, and a premium version with additional features, such as more profile insights and the ability to see who viewed your profile.

## The Pay-Per-Use Modelüí∞
Charge users each time they use the AI application or for specific services or features.
### Example
### Adobe Creative Cloud
Adobe offers its suite of creative software, including Photoshop, Illustrator, and Premiere Pro, on a pay-per-use model through its Creative Cloud service
### Google Cloud Platform
Google's cloud computing platform offers a range of services, including compute, storage, and machine learning, on a pay-per-use model.
### Salesforce
Salesforce is a popular customer relationship management (CRM) platform that offers a pay-per-use pricing model for its various services, including sales, marketing, and customer service.
